James joined St. Vincent St. Mary's High School in Akron, where he became a starter in his first year of playing. The team achieved a record of 27 wins and 0 losses. NBA period

LeBron James was selected by the Cleveland Cavaliers with the No. 1 overall pick in the 2003 NBA draft, and won the NBA regular season Most Valuable Player (MVP) in 2009 and 2010. In 2010, James moved to the Miami Heat. In 2012, James won the NBA's third regular season MVP, first championship and finals MVP, and won the London Olympic gold medal on behalf of the US men's basketball team, tying the record set by Michael Jordan in 1992.
